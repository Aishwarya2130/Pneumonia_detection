Pneumonia is a critical respiratory infection that inflames the air sacs in oneor bothlungs, potentially leading to life-threatening complications if not identifiedandtreated early. Chest X-rays are commonly used for its diagnosis, but interpretingthem accurately requires experienced radiologists and can be subject tohumanerror or diagnostic delays, especially in underserved regions. Recent advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) and deep learning haveopenedup new avenues for automating medical image analysis. Amongthese, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have shown remarkable performanceindetecting abnormalities from imaging data. This project focuses onbuildingacustom CNN model capable of automatically detecting pneumonia fromchest X-rayimages. The system is trained using a publicly available chest X-ray dataset, whichincludeslabeled images of normal and pneumonia-affected lungs. The CNNmodel iscarefully designed and tuned to extract relevant visual features fromthelungregions and classify the images with high accuracy. The entire workflow—fromimage preprocessing to model training, evaluation, and prediction—is built usingPython and TensorFlow/Keras. The final goal is to develop a scalable and lightweight AI-powered diagnostictool
that can assist healthcare professionals in faster and more reliable pneumoniadetection, thereby improving clinical outcomes and reducing diagnostic overhead.
